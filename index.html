Shortwave Radio Oddities

A List of Mysterious Broadcasts from Around the World

  • The Buzzer (UVB-76) - 4625 kHz - A repeating buzzing sound that pauses for voice transmissions in Russian.
  • The Pip - 5448 kHz, 3756 kHz - Emits a beeping sound with occasional Russian voice messages.
  • Lincolnshire Poacher - 5.4 MHz, 7.4 MHz, 9.4 MHz - Played a British folk tune before numbers.
  • Swedish Rhapsody - 3–10 MHz - Numbers read by a childlike voice, preceded by a Swedish folk tune.
  • Yankee Hotel Foxtrot (E10) - 5.879 MHz, 6.043 MHz - Known for phonetic alphabet transmissions.
  • Atención (Cuban Numbers Station) - 5.820 MHz, 6.890 MHz - Spanish numbers believed to be Cuban intelligence.
  • Squeaky Wheel - 5473 kHz, 3828 kHz - Squeaky tones and Russian messages, thought to be military.
  • Gong Station (XPH) - 3.903 MHz, 4.725 MHz - Musical gong followed by numeric codes.
  • Polish Counting Stations - 3–9 MHz - Female voice reading Polish numbers, speculated intelligence.
  • V07 “Bulgarian Betty” - 3–10 MHz - Numbers read in an Eastern European accent.
  • EZI/4XZ Israeli Naval Station - 4.819 MHz - Morse code communications for Israeli naval operations.
  • Tyrolean Music Station - 3.3–5 MHz - Played Tyrolean music followed by German number sequences.
  • The Clicker - 4625 kHz - Mechanical clicking sound, possibly Eastern European origin.
  • XP/XPA Polish Morse Code - 4–6 MHz - Numbers in Morse code, potentially from Polish intelligence.
  • Whale Songs - Varied frequencies, near 8.011 MHz - Strange, whale-like tones.
  • The Star-Spangled Banner Station - 5–9 MHz - Played the U.S. national anthem with encoded sequences.
  • UVB-76’s Spooky Twin - Around 4625 kHz - Similar to UVB-76 with pulsing tones and voice.
  • Windy (Russian Channel Marker) - 3.675 MHz - Wind-like noise with Morse code intermittently.
  • E11 Oblique - 3.3–9 MHz - Digital tones followed by English numbers.
  • The Gong-and-Morse (S06s) - 3–8 MHz - Gong sounds followed by Morse code.
  • Ghost of the ‘80s - Sporadic, unconfirmed - Played 1980s pop songs mixed with Morse code.
  • The Skylark - 4–9 MHz - Alternating traditional Chinese music and codes, military-related.
  • The New Star Station - 6.330 MHz - Chimes followed by Mandarin numbers.
  • XJT “The Jammer” - Near other broadcasts - Rapid jamming signal.
  • Noise Jockey - Varied, unconfirmed - Random noises, considered rogue transmissions.
